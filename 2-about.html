OUR STORY

An outgrowth of McGill University’s Centre for Intelligent Machines,
Independent Robotics is built on a foundation of AI capabilities, machine learning, neural networks, and vision processing.
The company remains an active member of the global community
of researchers pushing the boundaries of robotics.

Meet Gregory Dudek

Gregory Dudek is a chaired professor of computer science at McGill University in Montreal, Greg is the Director of McGill’s School of Computer Science and previously served as Director of the McGill Center for Intelligent Machines. A prolific researcher, he has published over 250 refereed articles on computer vision and robotics and is co-author of the book Computational Principles of Mobile Robotics. Greg is also a member of the Mila research institute, created to inspire innovation in the development of AI for the benefit of society.

Meet Martin Stanley

Martin Stanley has many years of business experience founding, investing in, growing and managing businesses. He has extensive expertise in the global technology marketplace, specifically all facets of building and managing technology enterprises including startup, product management, product development, OEM channels, web deployment, marketing operations, financing, acquisitions and divestitures.
A successful entrepreneur, for ten years he has served as co-CEO of Independent Robotics (IR), a specialist in custom hardware and software solutions and the creator of the AQUA2 autonomous underwater vehicle (AUV). As co-CEO of IR, he was also responsible for structuring growth investment financing for the company.

Meet Michael Jenkin

Michael Jenkin is a professor of computer science and engineering and a member of the Centre for Vision Research at York University, Canada. Working in the fields of visually guided autonomous robots and virtual reality, he has published over 150 research papers including co-authoring Computational Principles of Mobile Robotics with Gregory Dudek and a series of co-edited books on human and machine vision with Laurence Harris.

Meet Chris Prahacs

Chris Prahacs is the company’s senior robotics and systems engineer responsible for hardware development. After spending a number of years in working in the motorsport industry, Chris joined the University of Oxford's Mobile Robotics Group in 2012, where he was responsible for kitting out MRG's RobotCar, the first self-driving vehicle to run autonomously on public roads in the UK. As Oxford's MRG expanded to become the Oxford Robotics Institute, he became the lead hardware engineer responsible for all of the Institute's robots.
Previously, Chris worked in the Ambulatory Robotics Lab and the Mobile Robotics Lab at McGill University’s Centre for Intelligent Machines (CIM), where he was responsible for the design and construction of IR’s AQUA robots. He additionally was an adviser to undergraduate design teams completing their capstone year design course and was the faculty adviser for McGill’s Formula SAE team.
Chris holds a mechanical engineering degree from McGill, with a specialization in Design.
